ISKON TEMPLE












The ISKCON Temple in Ahmadnagar is part of the global network of temples affiliated with the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), which was founded by A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada in 1966. ISKCON is dedicated to the worship of Lord Krishna and the promotion of Bhakti Yoga (the path of devotion), primarily through the chanting of the “Hare Krishna” mantra and the practice of devotional service. The roots of ISKCON can be traced to the Gaudiya Vaishnavism tradition, a movement started by Chaitanya Mahaprabhu in the 16th century. However, the modern-day ISKCON movement began in the United States in the 1960s, when A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada took the message of Lord Krishna and Bhakti Yoga to the West, with the aim of spreading the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita, the Srimad Bhagavatam, and other sacred texts.The ISKCON Temple in Ahmadnagar plays an essential role in providing a space for worship, learning, and spiritual development in the region. Through its activities, it fosters a deep connection with Lord Krishna and promotes the core values of love, compassion, and devotion that are central to the Hare Krishna movement. It serves as a beacon of spiritual growth and a testament to the global reach of ISKCON's philosophy, enriching the lives of its devotees and spreading the message of peace and divine love.
